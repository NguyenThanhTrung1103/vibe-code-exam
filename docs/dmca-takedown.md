# DMCA Takedown Procedure

**Last updated:** 2026-04-30

We take copyright seriously. If you believe content on the Exam
Platform infringes your copyright, send a takedown notice to:

**dmca@exam.example.com** (placeholder — staffed by a real person from
public soft-launch onward; until then, internal-beta operator).

## Required information

A valid takedown notice must include:

1. **Identification of the copyrighted work** you claim has been
   infringed (title, ISBN/SKU, exam name, original URL).
2. **Identification of the infringing material** on this Service —
   include the question id (visible in the admin URL) or the exam +
   topic + first 30 characters of the question.
3. **Your contact information** — name, postal address, telephone,
   email.
4. **A statement** that you have a good-faith belief that the use is
   not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
5. **A statement, under penalty of perjury**, that the information in
   the notice is accurate and that you are authorised to act on
   behalf of the copyright owner.
6. **Your physical or electronic signature.**

## What we do on receipt

1. Acknowledge within **2 business days**.
2. Pull the material to **private** status (Phase 06 retire workflow)
   while we investigate — we do not auto-delete attempt history.
3. Notify the admin who imported the material (audit log lookup).
4. Within **10 business days**, either:
   * Permanently retire the content, or
   * Restore it with a **counter-notice** if the import attestation
     and source are valid.

## Counter-notice

If you imported the content and believe the takedown was issued in
error, you may submit a counter-notice with the same fields as above
plus a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the relevant
court. We forward the counter-notice to the original claimant.

## Repeat-infringer policy

Admins who file three substantiated takedown notices against their
imports lose admin import privileges. Service users who file
fraudulent counter-notices are banned.

## Internal-beta note

During Phase 1 internal beta, all imports come from the operator.
External-author uploads are not enabled. The DMCA contact is
maintained anyway so the procedure is in place for Phase 2.
